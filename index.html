Hydrangea Care Guide

Soil for Hydrangea

Well-draining soil with organic matter.

Hydrangeas prefer soil that drains well and is enriched with organic matter.

Watering

Regular watering, keeping the soil consistently moist.

Hydrangeas thrive with consistent moisture, especially during hot periods.

Pruning

Prune to control size and remove spent blooms.

Pruning helps maintain the shape and encourages new growth.

Colors

Benefits

Large, showy blooms in various colors.

Hydrangeas are known for their stunning and diverse flower heads.

Adaptable to different garden styles.

They work well as shrubs, hedges, or in mixed borders.

Non-Benefits

Flower color may vary with soil pH; alkaline soil produces pink blooms, acidic soil produces blue blooms.
